What is "Google"?

Google is an Internet search engine developed by our US company Google LLC. The search engine enables users to search the World Wide Web for information of all kinds. Founded in 1998, Google has developed into one of the world's leading search engines and technology companies.

Our main goal is to help users find relevant and high quality information on the Internet. In addition to traditional web search, Google offers a variety of services and products, including Maps, Gmail, YouTube and many more. These services help to simplify users' daily lives and make information easily accessible.

Where did it all begin?

The origins of Google date back to 1995 at Stanford University. Larry Page was considering studying at the prestigious university and during his deliberations, Sergey Brin, who was already enrolled there as a student, was asked to show him around the campus. It was in the midst of this student encounter that the fascinating story began that would make the two of them pioneers of the Internet world.

Larry Page and Sergey Brin had a shared vision that went beyond the campus tour. Their pursuit of knowledge and innovation led to the development of a search engine that had the potential to revolutionise the internet. In 1996, they began collaborating on a project called "Backrub", which focused on analysing backlinks to evaluate the relevance of websites.

The first steps had been taken and the two founders began to refine their ideas. The company Google Inc. was finally founded in 1998. The name "Google" is an allusion to the mathematical concept "googol", which stands for the almost infinite amount of information that they wanted to make accessible through their search engine.

Where is Google's headquarters located?

The Googleplex, located at 1600 Amphitheatre Parkway in Mountain View, California, serves as the central headquarters for Google and its parent company, Alphabet Inc. This imposing campus spans an expansive area and represents the centrepiece of the technological innovations that drive the companies forward.

With its architectural diversity, modern working environments and innovative design concepts, the Googleplex creates an inspiring atmosphere for the creative minds who work there. In addition to office buildings, the campus also includes numerous amenities, including cafeterias, recreational areas, gyms and green open spaces that promote the work-life balance of employees.

The Googleplex is not only a physical location, but also a symbol of Google and Alphabet's mission to improve the world through technology. This is where ideas are born, innovations are fostered and projects are driven forward that have the potential to revolutionise the way we live, work and communicate.
